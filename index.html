Jaromir Jagr

BIO

Early Life

Born in what was Czechoslovakia, Jaromir started his hockey career from a young age, playing at the highest level in the Czech Republic at the age of just 15. He would end up being the 5th overall pick in the 1990 draft, and was the longest remaining player from that draft.

NHL Career

Jagr played for many teams including Calgary Flames, Pittsburgh Penguins, New York Rangers, Washington Capitals, and the Dallas Stars to name a few. He won the Stanely cup twice, in '91 and '92, back to back while with the Penguins. Jagr won the Art Ross Trophy in '95, a trophy received by some of the greatest players in the history of the game like Wayne Gretzky and Mario Lemieux. His career continued moving from team to team and lasted 28 long years. He is one of the most respected players of all time.

Post NHL

As of this year, Jagr has left the NHL to play for his hometown team where he started HC Klando in the Czech Republic. His hockey plans are indefinite.
